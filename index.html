The Center at a Glance

Overview & Mission

Founded in 2016 at The American University of Paris, the Center for Critical Democracy Studies advances interdisciplinary inquiry into democracy as a social state, political regime, and form of collective agency.

Mission

The Center for Critical Democracy Studies pursues inquiry into the emancipatory possibilities of democracy in the widest sense. We encourage work that understands democracy as a way of organizing social relations based on equality and as a means of self-governing toward individual and collective liberty.

Visiting Scholars Program

Senior and junior researchers interested in applying are welcome to submit at any time. Scholars have access to CCDS resources, library privileges, and participation in CCDS seminars. Visiting scholars are encouraged to present their research, organize events, and collaborate with CCDS members and the AUP community.

Required documents: CV · selected publications list · proposed stay dates (minimum 3 months notice) · brief research description · potential collaborations · financing information. For PhD candidates: a recommendation letter.

Apply · ccds@aup.edu

Law, State, Democracy (LSD)
Promoting an interdisciplinary approach to questions of law, state and democracy
LSD seeks to shed new light on the interdependence and fundamental co-construction of law and democracy in promoting just and effective modes of government. Launched December 2025 with an inaugural international symposium of 19 speakers from 2 partner institutions.

COntinuous COnstruction of resilient social COntracts through societal transformations
CO3 studies more open, inclusive, and democratic models of social contracts. The project creates resilient societies capable of addressing societal challenges and anti-democratic tendencies. CCDS share: €273k of €3.0M consortium.

OppAttune
Countering Oppositional Political Extremism through Attuned Dialogue
OppAttune developed an innovative Attunement Model to track and limit extreme political narratives in "everyday extremism" — polarising issues in common-sense discussions. Examined 6 national case studies. CCDS share: €269k of €2.09M consortium.

D.Rad — De-Radicalization in Europe and Beyond
Detect, Resolve, Re-integrate
D.Rad identified factors leading to radicalization and developed recommendations to counter extremism, reintegrate radicalised individuals, and improve social inclusion. Drew on machine learning, discourse analysis, and social psychology. CCDS share: €255k of €3.1M consortium. Produced 8 policy reports including 6 on radicalization in France, and a landmark art exhibition.

Part VI

Education · Engaging Students

The Center integrates democratic inquiry into AUP's liberal arts curriculum through experimental courses, student competitions, and international delegations.

Democracy Lab
Semester-long design-thinking courses where students identify a democratic challenge and devise a prototype solution. Inspired by John Dewey's pragmatist pedagogy.
Education JusticeRadical ViolenceGlobal CitizenshipPrison Education
4 thematic editions since 2020
Prison Education Workshop
AUP students share readings, discussions, and creative activities with detainees at La Santé prison in Paris, using circle pedagogy and egalitarian approaches. Led by Dr. Hannah Taieb; non-profit partner: Dialogue & Transformation.
7 iterations · Fall 2019 – Summer 2024
Tocqueville Challenge
Student competition organized by the Tocqueville Foundation. CCDS-supervised teams develop public interest projects; winners receive funding to implement their solutions.
3 projects supervised · 2022–2023
Tocqueville Conversations
Annual international conference at the Château de Tocqueville (Normandy). CCDS selects outstanding AUP students to attend alongside ~200 politicians, intellectuals, and civil society actors.
4 editions 2022–2025 · 8 AUP student delegates
Athens Democracy Forum
Since 2016, AUP nominates two students each year as Ambassadors to the Athens Democracy Forum, organized by the Democracy & Culture Foundation. Ambassadors join the Global Liberal Arts Alliance delegation.
up to 2 ambassadors per year · since 2016
Model United Nations (AUPMUN)
Student-led organization founded in 1985. Weekly sessions simulating UN structures; delegations to international MUN conferences across Europe.
Highest-awarded delegation · Barcelona 2025
