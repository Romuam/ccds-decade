The Center at a Glance

Overview & Mission

Founded in 2016 at The American University of Paris, the Center for Critical Democracy Studies advances interdisciplinary inquiry into democracy as a social state, political regime, and form of collective agency.

Mission

The Center for Critical Democracy Studies pursues inquiry into the emancipatory possibilities of democracy in the widest sense. We encourage work that understands democracy as a way of organizing social relations based on equality and as a means of self-governing toward individual and collective liberty.

Team Profiles
Stephen W. Sawyer
Director · Ballantine-Leavitt Professor of History
Stephen W. Sawyer is the Ballantine-Leavitt Professor of History and Director of the Center for Critical Democracy Studies. He came to AUP from the University of Chicago center in Paris and the École Normale Supérieure–rue d'Ulm. After receiving fellowships from the EHESS, Fulbright, and Sciences Po, Sawyer served as part-time assistant to Pierre Rosanvallon at the Collège de France.
Roman Zinigrad
Deputy Director · Assistant Professor of Law
Roman Zinigrad is Assistant Professor of Law and, from 2026, Deputy Director of the Center for Critical Democracy Studies. He coordinates the AUP History, Law & Society undergraduate program.
Julian Culp
Faculty Fellow · Associate Professor of Philosophy
Julian Culp is Associate Professor of Philosophy, Program Coordinator for Philosophy, and Fellow of the Center for Critical Democracy Studies. Previously, he was a lecturer at the Goethe University of Frankfurt am Main, where he received his Habilitation and PhD. He held postdoctoral fellowships from the University of Toronto and the Catholic University of Louvain, and research stays at Duke and Princeton.
Mirjam Dageförde
Faculty Fellow · Assistant Professor of Comparative Politics
Mirjam Dageförde researches failing representation, citizen-politics relations, party politics, and inequality with a focus on Europe — France and Germany in particular. She is currently Assistant Professor of Comparative Politics at AUP and an Associate Researcher at Sciences Po Paris and the WZB (Wissenschaftszentrum Berlin).
Miranda Spieler
Faculty Fellow · Professor of History
Professor Miranda Spieler joined the faculty of AUP in 2012. She holds an AB from Harvard College and a PhD from Columbia University, where she also served as assistant to writer Susan Sontag. Before joining AUP she was in the history department at the University of Arizona, where she received tenure in 2011.
Nathanaël Colin-Jaeger
Affiliate Member · Maître de Conférences, ESPOL
Nathanaël Colin-Jaeger is Maître de Conférences (Associate Professor) of Politics, Philosophy & Economics at ESPOL (European School of Political and Social Sciences, Catholic University of Lille). He is Affiliate Member at the CCDS and was Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Center from January 2024 to June 2025.
Annabelle Lever
Affiliate Member · Professor of Political Philosophy, Sciences Po
Annabelle Lever is an Affiliate Member of the CCDS since September 2025 and Professor of Political Philosophy at Sciences Po, Paris. She writes mainly on democratic theory, ethics, and public policy.
Zona Zarić
Affiliate Member · Research Fellow, IFDT Belgrade
Zona Zarić, philosopher and feminist, organises the Center's "Care & Democracy" seminar series and previously held a CCDS postdoctoral fellowship during which she contributed to the OppAttune project. She holds a PhD in Political Philosophy from the ENS-Ulm, a J.D. in law from the University of Belgrade, and an MA from AUP.
Naintara Maya Oberoi
Postdoctoral Research Fellow
Naintara Maya Oberoi is a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the CCDS since October 2025. She completed her doctoral research in political philosophy at the École Normale Supérieure, Paris, specialising in questions of subalternity and citizenship.
Hannah Davis Taieb
Clinical Fellow · PhD in Anthropology
Dr. Hannah Davis Taieb is a Clinical Fellow at the Center for Critical Democracy Studies. She holds a PhD in anthropology and is the founder and leader of AUP's innovative prison education workshops, where students learn from and with people who are currently incarcerated.
Alice Crary
Visiting Scholar 2025–26 · New School
Alice Crary is a moral and social philosopher, currently Walter A. Eberstadt Distinguished University Professor at the New School for Social Research and Visiting Fellow in Philosophy and Christian Ethics at Regent's Park College, Oxford. She is also a Researcher-in-Residence at Maison Suger (FMSH) and Visiting Researcher at ISJPS (Sorbonne/Paris 1).
Juliet A. Williams
Visiting Scholar 2025–26 · UCLA
Juliet A. Williams is Professor of Gender Studies and Faculty Director of the UCLA Quarter in Washington Program. She holds a B.A. in Government from Harvard and a Ph.D. in Government from Cornell. She is a socio-legal scholar specialising in feminist theory and politics, masculinities studies, gender discrimination law, and Title IX.
Margaret Crosby
Visiting Scholar 2026–27 · Liberty University
Margaret Crosby is a Senior Deputy Prosecutor with extensive experience at the intersection of trauma, victimization, and institutional response. She brings expertise in law and society, with professional experience as a senior deputy prosecutor. Her work combines practical legal knowledge with scholarly interests in democratic justice and institutional reform.
Jennifer Ritchie
Visiting Clinical Scholar · Seattle, WA
Jennifer Ritchie is a Senior Deputy Prosecutor with the King County Prosecuting Attorney's Office in Seattle, where she has spent over thirty years working at the intersection of trauma, victimization, and institutional response. She served nearly a decade as Commissioner on the Washington State Supreme Court Commission on Gender and Justice.
Shane McLorrain
Tocqueville 21 Editor
Shane McLorrain is a Visiting Research Analyst with the Center for Critical Democracy Studies and an Editor for Tocqueville 21. His contributions focus on geopolitics, international political economy, and French politics. He is also the creator, host, and producer of the Tocqueville 21 Podcast.

Visiting Scholars Program

Senior and junior researchers interested in applying are welcome to submit at any time. Scholars have access to CCDS resources, library privileges, and participation in CCDS seminars. Visiting scholars are encouraged to present their research, organize events, and collaborate with CCDS members and the AUP community.

Required documents: CV · selected publications list · proposed stay dates (minimum 3 months notice) · brief research description · potential collaborations · financing information. For PhD candidates: a recommendation letter.

Apply · ccds@aup.edu

Law, State, Democracy (LSD)
Promoting an interdisciplinary approach to questions of law, state and democracy
LSD seeks to shed new light on the interdependence and fundamental co-construction of law and democracy in promoting just and effective modes of government. Launched December 2025 with an inaugural international symposium of 19 speakers from 2 partner institutions.

OppAttune
Countering Oppositional Political Extremism through Attuned Dialogue
OppAttune developed an innovative Attunement Model to track and limit extreme political narratives in "everyday extremism" — polarising issues in common-sense discussions. Examined 6 national case studies. CCDS share: €269k of €2.09M consortium.

D.Rad — De-Radicalization in Europe and Beyond
Detect, Resolve, Re-integrate
D.Rad identified factors leading to radicalization and developed recommendations to counter extremism, reintegrate radicalised individuals, and improve social inclusion. Drew on machine learning, discourse analysis, and social psychology. CCDS share: €255k of €3.1M consortium. Produced 8 policy reports including 6 on radicalization in France, and a landmark art exhibition.

Part VI

Education · Engaging Students

The Center integrates democratic inquiry into AUP's liberal arts curriculum through experimental courses, student competitions, and international delegations.

Democracy Lab
Semester-long design-thinking courses where students identify a democratic challenge and devise a prototype solution. Inspired by John Dewey's pragmatist pedagogy.
Education JusticeRadical ViolenceGlobal CitizenshipPrison Education
4 thematic editions since 2020
Prison Education Workshop
AUP students share readings, discussions, and creative activities with detainees at La Santé prison in Paris, using circle pedagogy and egalitarian approaches. Led by Dr. Hannah Taieb; non-profit partner: Dialogue & Transformation.
7 iterations · Fall 2019 – Summer 2024
Tocqueville Challenge
Student competition organized by the Tocqueville Foundation. CCDS-supervised teams develop public interest projects; winners receive funding to implement their solutions.
3 projects supervised · 2022–2023
Tocqueville Conversations
Annual international conference at the Château de Tocqueville (Normandy). CCDS selects outstanding AUP students to attend alongside ~200 politicians, intellectuals, and civil society actors.
4 editions 2022–2025 · 8 AUP student delegates
Athens Democracy Forum
Since 2016, AUP nominates two students each year as Ambassadors to the Athens Democracy Forum, organized by the Democracy & Culture Foundation. Ambassadors join the Global Liberal Arts Alliance delegation.
up to 2 ambassadors per year · since 2016
Model United Nations (AUPMUN)
Student-led organization founded in 1985. Weekly sessions simulating UN structures; delegations to international MUN conferences across Europe.
